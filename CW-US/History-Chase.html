Places to visit

Washington D.C.

One could say that Washington D.C. is the heart of the United States as it is home to numerous historical monuments and legislative buildings that have a rich background all their own.

Mount Rushmore, SD

Originally commisioned in 1941 by Gutzon Borglum this sculpture depicts some of the most relevant and impactful presidents in America's past. This site also features a museum inside with interactive exhibits that reveal and display the history of the monument.

The Alamo, TX

The Alamo refers to a building that the battleground for one of the most gruesome battles during Texas's war for indepedence from Mexico. Despite valiantly holding the position fornearly two weeks the fort eventually fell and was used as a rallying cry to reinvigorate the remaining forces by saying "Rembember the Alamo".

NASA headquarters and mission control for most manned exploration of space holds a deep past and offers many exhibits and attractions that bring the life the race to space and difficulties thereafter.
