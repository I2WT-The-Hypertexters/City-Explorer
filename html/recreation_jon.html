Places to visit:



Calgary Stampede

This 10-day affair is one among the foremost widely anticipated events in Western Canada, with many locals and summertime travelers planning their holidays around the Calgary Stampede. For in the week in July, the town of Calgary turns into a real western town, where people that might rather be wearing suits to go instead with jeans and cowboy boots.

Thompson Pool Hotel, Toronto

With breathtaking 360° views of the downtown Toronto skyline and Lake Ontario , Thompson Toronto has the city’s premier rooftop bar retreat for hotel guests and lounge members. Guests can dip within the infinity pool by day and lounge in by night, enjoying creative cocktails far above the fray of downtown Toronto.

Whistler Blackcomb

Whistler-Blackcomb combines the terrain of two mountains to become the most important resort in North America. With over 200 runs, serviced by 37 lifts, Whistler boasts 8,172 ac of skiable terrain which will accommodate everyone from first-timer skiers to extreme skiers. The resort is definitely rated one among the simplest ski resorts within the world and offers a variety of terrain from inbounds backcountry, bowls, glaciers, tree trails to five terrain parks! Whistler village also has an abundance of accommodation from condos to luxury hotels, packed along a pedestrian-only path lined with great dining experiences, shopping boutiques and art galleries.

Ice Skating Trail at Arrowhead Provincial Park

Arrowhead Provincial Park’s new skating trail may have generated the park tons of attention, but the reality is that they have long been renowned as Muskoka’s winter playground. The provincial park is considered one among the highest race ski destinations in Ontario, with 28km of classic ski trails and 16km of skate ski trails, ranging in difficulty From beginner to expert.
