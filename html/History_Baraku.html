Places to visit:



Stonehenge

One of the most mysterious monuments in existance, built around 5000 years ago. Located in Willtshire, this monument is the spawn of countless theories and tales. Since there are no written records, the reason of construction remains a mystery. One of the most popular theories is that the stones were built by the Druids, high priests of the Celts, for sacrificial ceremonies. These stones weight from 4 to 30 tons and were dragged from 40 to 300 kilometers. This prehistoric monument attracts over a million visitors each year and is part of the UNESCO World Herritage Site.

9:30 AM - 5:00 PM (22€)

Tower of London

The Tower of London was built by William the Conqueror in 1066 as a fortification near the Thames River. Over the years it has served as a royal palace, fortress and prison. The Tower took 20 years of construction and stretches for more than 7 hecktares. Be part of the 2 million yearly visitors by taking a tour that includes the Bloody Tower, Medieval Palace, White Tower, The Crown Jewels and much more!

10:00 AM - 3:00 PM (28€)

British Museum

Discover two million years of culture and history in one of the largest museums in the world! With a collection of over eight million works, the museum represents the history of human culture from its beginnings to the present. The British Museum was established in 1753 and first opened to the public six years later. British colonisation aided its expansion over the years and it contains works from six continents. Some collections are available online through their interactive website!

10:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Free)

Where to stay

Castle Hotel



Take the opportunity to stay at one of the many castles turned into hotels in England, as surreal as that sounds!



Sleeping in a castle is an unforgettable stay that will take you back hundreds of years.



"Coombe Abbey Hotel" is a hotel built on a preserved abbey that dates back to the 12th-century and offers rooms for around 136€, breakfast included.
