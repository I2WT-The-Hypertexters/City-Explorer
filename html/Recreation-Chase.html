The Recreationalist

The Recreationalist likes to enjoy things at their own pace. For them, life isn't a race--so why not smell the roses on the way?

They enjoy more casual activities such as trying new food or seeing attractions not for their historical or pictorial properties but rather their entertainment value.

Seeing a play or participating in a parade are just a few examples of such attractions. They enjoy travel to get away from the busyness of life and to experience new activities, but at a slower pace than an Adventurer, for example.

They don't mind historic locations, but they strongly dislike the popular tourist areas or more extreme activities. Recreationalist tends to be a bit older in mind and or body which means they have probably been another type of traveler at one point and have realized it was not all it was cracked up to be.

Things to do



Las Vegas, NV

Don't let the name Sin City fool you, Las Vegas is a great place to relax outside of the bustling bars and clubs. Many casinos are quiet enviornments that offer low stakes entertainment while you eat or talk with others! There is also a variety of shows and comedy clubs that encourage a more laid back attitude. There are many famous locations from TV here as well such as the shop from Pawn Stars.

New York City, NY

NYC is one of the most famous cities in the world for a reason! With one of the most dense populations in the United States you could be forgiven for assuming the hustle of everyday life of NYC would drive away the average Recreationalist but you'd be mistaken. NYC is home to many attractions right up the ally of someone wanting to simply enjoy themselves without too much stress. A prime example of this is Broadway theatre! Another might be the Empire State Building or even the Statue of Liberty!

Yellow Stone National Park, WY

National Parks are a great place for Recreationalists as they offer a unique escape back into experiencing nature on a daily basis. This location was the world's first national park and earned its popularity through the "Old Faithful" geyser and "Grand Prismatic" spring. Great for exploration Yellowstone has the largest gathering of active geysers in the world! Not only that, but it is home to many uncommon animals such as grizzly bears, wolves, and bison. These lovable mammals can be found sauntering around making this a great place to watch the protected wildlife.

Maui, HI

Out of all the islands in Hawaii, Maui is the most laid back--and that's saying something. Maui offers many activities such as surfing, tours around the entire island, and even viewing of whales! The cuisine is usually locally sourced and the beaches are beautiful which makes Maui the perfect away for any Recreationalist!
