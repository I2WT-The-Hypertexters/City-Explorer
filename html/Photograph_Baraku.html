Places to visit:



Big Ben

This iconic london landmark is located at the north of the Westminister Palace. The tower stands 96m tall and was built in 1843. To get the best view of the tower, it is recommended to visit in the evenings, when the tower lights up. When visiting the Big Ben, you are surrounded by breathtaking views that include the Westminister Bridge and Thames River. To take some great pictures you can also go on a ride on the London Eye, for a price of 30€.

Chester Zoo

With over 35 000 animals and 50 hectares of zoological gardens, Chester Zoo is the largest zoo in England. The zoo was opened in 1931 and its the most visited wildife attraction in Britain, with over 2 million yearly visitors. In 2007 Forbes included Chester Zoo as one of the fifteen best zoos in the world, while in 2017 it was named third in the world by TripAdvisor. Take the opportunity to meet and photograph some of the most exotic animals in the planet. Entrance to the zoo costs 26€.

Jurastic Coast

The Jurassic Coast, a World Heritage Site, is located in the coast of southern England. This geographical documents 185 million years of geological history. The coast stretches for 154 km and makes up for some phenomenal nature sights, more famously the Durdle Door. This natural arch, formed from a layer of hard limestone, stands vertically out of the sea.
